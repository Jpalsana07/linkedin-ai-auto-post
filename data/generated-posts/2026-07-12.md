# LinkedIn Post

Topic: SaaS Build-in-Public Metrics

Date: 2026-07-12

---

I hid our revenue numbers.

We were building a SaaS in public,
and I thought the point was to look confident while we found product-market fit.

So I posted screenshots of signups,
but left out churn, refunds, and the ugly weeks.

The problem was simple:
people could see momentum, but not truth.

Inside the team, it got worse.
We started optimizing for the numbers that looked good in screenshots.
More trials.
More top-of-funnel.
Less attention on who actually stuck around.

When we finally shared the full picture in Notion,
a simple doc tool,
the story changed fast.

One month later, the team stopped asking,
“How do we make this look better?”
and started asking,
“How do we make this worth paying for?”

The lesson I keep coming back to:
if you build in public, publish the numbers that can hurt you.

That’s the only version that teaches you what the market is really saying.

What metric would you be most tempted to hide?

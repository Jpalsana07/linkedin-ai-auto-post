Topic: Node.js at Scale

Date: 2026-06-29

---

Node.js is not the problem.

The hot take: your team is probably using it badly.

The dominant story is simple: Node.js is for startups, then you "graduate" to something safer when traffic grows.
That sounds neat.
It also made us overcomplicate a service that only needed to stay fast, cheap, and easy to change.

We had one API on Node 18 serving about 2.3 million requests a day.
The latency spikes were real, but the root cause was not Node.
It was 14 different middlewares, too much JSON copying, and a queue that nobody owned.

We trimmed the stack.
Removed 6 middlewares.
Stopped parsing the same payload twice.
Kept the process boring.

p95 latency dropped from 480ms to 140ms in a week.

The strongest argument against this is fair: Node can fall over when you mix heavy CPU work with lots of traffic.
That is true.
If your app is doing image processing or long-running computation, Node is the wrong fit.

But most "Node doesn’t scale" stories I’ve seen were really "our architecture didn’t scale."

The lesson: scale the system before you blame the language.

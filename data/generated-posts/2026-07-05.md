MCP is not the bottleneck.

Everyone keeps talking like the model is brilliant and the missing piece is just better tools.
Or better context.
Or a smarter way to wire everything together.

I built a small support bot with Claude and MCP, the Model Context Protocol that lets an AI call tools in a standard way.
The bot could search tickets, pull orders, and draft replies.

It still sounded lost.

The problem was not access.
It was what I was feeding it, and when.

I spent 2 days shrinking a 14-page context dump into 6 short facts the bot actually needed.
Same tools.
Same model.
Far better answers.

That was the moment context engineering clicked for me:
most AI failures are not “the bot can’t do it.”
They’re “the bot was given the wrong shape of information.”

The strongest counterargument is real: MCP matters because it stops every team from building custom glue for every app.
That is true.
Standard plumbing is useful.

But once the pipes work, the hard part is still deciding what goes through them.

Less context, better chosen, beats more context every time.

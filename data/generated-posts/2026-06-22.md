# LinkedIn Post

Topic: SaaS Build-in-Public Metrics

Date: 2026-06-22

---

My metrics post embarrassed me.

I was building a SaaS dashboard for founders.
It tracked signups, trials, and paid conversions.
Then I shared weekly numbers publicly, and the story got ugly.

The traffic looked healthy.
The pipeline looked alive.
But 82% of signups never touched the product again.

I blamed the funnel at first.
So I rewired the onboarding, changed the email sequence, and added a mixpanel event tracker — a tool that logs user actions — to every screen.

Nothing moved.

The real problem was simpler.
I had been measuring curiosity, not commitment.
A blog reader clicking a pricing page made the dashboard look good.
A real customer opening the app twice told the truth.

That was the postmortem:
my build-in-public metrics were rewarding vanity, not traction.

The lesson I keep coming back to is this:
if a metric makes you feel busy instead of honest, it is probably lying to you.

What metric have you seen look impressive while hiding the real story?

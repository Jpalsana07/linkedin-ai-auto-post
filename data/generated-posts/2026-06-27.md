# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Freelancing with Numbers

Date: 2026-06-27

---

I priced work wrong.

I was freelancing on a 6-week client build.
The question was simple: charge by the hour, or charge one fixed price.

That choice changes everything.
Hourly is safer when the scope is blurry.
Fixed price is cleaner when the client wants a clear outcome.

I chose fixed price on purpose.

The tradeoff was real.
If I got fast, I kept the upside.
If I got surprised, I ate the cost.

What most people miss is cash flow.
Hourly can look better on paper.
But clients pay slower when they feel every extra meeting.
A fixed quote with a 50% upfront payment gave me predictability, and predictability mattered more than squeezing every extra dollar.

That mattered because I was solo.
No buffer.
No team to absorb a 2-week delay.

The lesson: pick the pricing model that protects the constraint you actually have, not the one that sounds fairest.

If you’ve freelanced, when did you choose certainty over upside?

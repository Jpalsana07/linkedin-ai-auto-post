Topic: Startup Lessons

Date: 2026-06-18

---

We shipped a broken migration.

It touched 18,000 customer records in one night.

The script looked fine in staging.
In production, one missing WHERE clause started rewriting the wrong rows.

Support lit up at 2:13 a.m.
Finance froze refunds.
Engineering spent 11 hours restoring backups from Postgres and re-running jobs by hand.

The cost was not just the incident.
We lost 3 days of roadmap time and a customer who had trusted us with their billing data.

The mistake wasn’t “moving too fast.”
It was shipping a change that could not prove it was safe before it touched real data.

That one still follows me.

Now, before any migration goes live, I want a dry run, a row count, and a rollback plan that a tired person can execute at 2 a.m.

The lesson: if a change can corrupt trust, the burden is on the code to prove it deserves prod.

What’s the worst production mistake that changed how you ship?

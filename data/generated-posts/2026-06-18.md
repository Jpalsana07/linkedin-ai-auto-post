Simple architecture beats clever architecture.

I learned that the hard way on a backend rewrite.

We had 14 services, gRPC between teams, and a beautiful diagram in Miro.
It looked like a senior engineer had painted it.

Then one small change took 11 days to ship.

The bottleneck wasn’t code.
It was coordination, versioning, and everyone being afraid to touch the seams.

We cut it down to 3 services and moved two workflows back into a single Rails app.
Not because microservices are bad.
Because our team was too small for the shape we’d built.

Deploys dropped from 6 steps to 1.
On-call pages fell by 40% in the next month.

The contrarian part:
architecture is not a trophy for maturity.
It’s a cost you pay every day.

The strongest argument for complexity is scale.
Sometimes that’s true.

But most teams I’ve seen don’t need more boundaries.
They need fewer places to fail.

What architecture choice looked smart on paper and slowed you down in practice?

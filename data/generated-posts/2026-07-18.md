# LinkedIn Post

Topic: MCP and Context Engineering

Date: 2026-07-18

---

We shipped the wrong context.

We were building an MCP server to let our support bot read tickets, fetch order data, and draft replies.
It had to cut first-response time for a queue that handled 4,000 tickets a week.

The model looked smart in demos.
Then it started pulling the wrong customer history and mixing up two people with the same first name.

The bug wasn’t the model.
It was our context engineering — the way we chose, trimmed, and labeled the information the model saw.

We had sent everything.
Too much chat history, too many fields, no clear priority.
The bot was guessing what mattered.

Once we changed the context to send only the last relevant ticket, the order ID, and one sentence of intent, the replies got boring in the best way.
Accurate.
Fast.
Consistent.

The lesson: with MCP, the model is rarely the problem.
Bad context turns a capable assistant into a confident liar.

What context do you think teams should stop sending first?

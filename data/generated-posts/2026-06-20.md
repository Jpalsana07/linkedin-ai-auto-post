# LinkedIn Post

Topic: React Shipping Stories

Date: 2026-06-20

---

We shipped the dashboard again.

It’s a React app for small ops teams who live in spreadsheets all day.

This week I rebuilt the slowest screen, the one that opens before the morning standup and decides whether people trust the product.

I cut the first load from 4.8 seconds to 1.9.

The change was boring in the best way.

I split one huge React component into smaller pieces, moved the heaviest chart behind a lazy load, and swapped a couple of “just render everything” habits for plain old loading states.

The weird part: the app looked almost the same.

But the behavior changed fast.

Support tickets about “the page froze” dropped from 11 last week to 3 this week.

What’s still broken?

Our filter bar is still doing too much work on every keystroke, and on older laptops you can feel it.

That’s the part I’m fixing next.

The lesson: users don’t care how elegant your React code feels.

They care about whether the screen appears before they give up.

What’s the slowest screen you’ve ever had to make feel fast?

# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Next.js in Production

Date: 2026-06-23

---

Next.js is overrated in prod.

The dominant story is simple:
use Next.js, ship faster, let the framework handle the hard parts.

That sounds right until your app gets real traffic.
Then you start caring about cache behavior, server costs, and pages that look fine in dev but feel sticky in production.

We learned this on a B2B app with about 40,000 monthly visits.
A single product page was taking 1.8 seconds to become useful because of server-side rendering on every request.
Not broken.
Just expensive in the wrong way.

We moved that page to static generation plus a small client fetch.
Same design.
Same copy.
Same users.

The page dropped under 400 ms.
Our AWS bill fell by 27% that month.

My contrarian take:
Next.js is great for speed of building, not always speed of running.

The strongest counterargument is real:
for teams with small apps, tight deadlines, and mixed frontend skills, Next.js is still one of the best defaults.
I agree.

But once traffic, latency, and cloud cost matter, the framework stops being the answer and starts being the tradeoff.

The lesson:
pick your stack for what it will cost you at scale, not how pleasant it feels on day one.

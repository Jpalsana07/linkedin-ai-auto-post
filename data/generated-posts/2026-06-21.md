# LinkedIn Post

Topic: MCP and Context Engineering

Date: 2026-06-21

---

MCP looked simple.

We were wiring an AI assistant to our internal tools.
The goal was basic: let it read tickets, check docs, and draft replies without a human copy-pasting everything.

The decision was where to keep the context.
MCP, the model context protocol, is the standard way a bot can ask for tools and data.
Context engineering is how you decide what the model actually sees.

The tradeoff was speed versus safety.

If we sent everything, the assistant felt smarter.
It also got slower, cost more, and started mixing old details with fresh ones.
If we sent too little, it missed the one line in a ticket that changed the answer.

We chose narrow context plus better retrieval.
Retrieval means fetching only the most relevant info.
That cut average response time from 8 seconds to 2.3 seconds, and the model stopped “helpfully” using stale notes.

The constraint most people miss is attention, not capability.
A model can only reason well about what fits in front of it.

This tradeoff does not hold when the task is exploratory, like research or debugging a messy incident.
Then wider context can be worth the noise.

What do you optimize first when wiring an AI to real tools: breadth or precision?

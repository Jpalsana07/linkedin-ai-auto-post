Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-06-04

This privacy policy describes how the LinkedIn AI Agent project (the “App”) handles data. The App is an open-source automation tool that generates one LinkedIn post per day using an LLM and publishes it to the authenticated user’s own LinkedIn account via LinkedIn’s official Posts API.

Who this policy is for

The App is built for individual operators running their own instance of the open-source code from the project repository. Each operator authorizes the App against their own LinkedIn account and is the sole user and beneficiary of that instance.

What data the App accesses

  • LinkedIn identity via OpenID Connect: your unique member identifier (the sub claim, used to build your urn:li:person:<id>), your name, and your locale.
  • LinkedIn posting permission via the w_member_social scope: used solely to publish posts to your own LinkedIn feed.
  • OAuth tokens (access token and, if your app is approved for it, refresh token): stored as environment variables / GitHub Secrets controlled exclusively by you. The App authors do not have access to your tokens.
  • Post history: a local data/history.json file inside your own repository, used to avoid duplicate or similar-sounding posts. This data never leaves your repository.

What the App does NOT do

  • Does not collect, store, or transmit your data to any third-party server operated by the App authors.
  • Does not read your LinkedIn connections, messages, posts, or any data outside the scopes listed above.
  • Does not sell, share, or monetize any user data in any form.
  • Does not use your data to train AI models.
  • Does not interact with other users’ LinkedIn accounts. Posts are published only to the authenticated operator’s own profile.

Third parties

To generate post text, the App sends a prompt to an LLM provider chosen by the operator: OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google. The prompt may include excerpts of the operator’s recent posts (from data/history.json) to help avoid repetition. No LinkedIn OAuth tokens are sent to these providers. Each provider’s own privacy policy governs how they handle that data.

Data retention

All persisted data lives inside the operator’s own GitHub repository or local filesystem. Operators control retention by editing or deleting data/history.json and data/generated-posts/. Revoking the App’s LinkedIn access from your LinkedIn data permissions page immediately invalidates the OAuth tokens.

Security

OAuth tokens are stored exclusively as environment variables or encrypted GitHub Secrets controlled by the operator. The App uses LinkedIn’s official OAuth 2.0 authorization-code flow with CSRF state verification. The App makes no network calls outside of: the chosen LLM provider’s API endpoint and api.linkedin.com.

Children

The App is not directed at children under 16 and does not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes to this policy

Any changes to this policy will be published at this URL and committed to the project repository. Material changes will update the effective date above.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised as a GitHub issue on the project repository, or sent to the email address listed on the operator’s LinkedIn Developer App.

This policy applies to the open-source App as published. Operators who modify the code and run derivative versions are responsible for their own data handling practices.

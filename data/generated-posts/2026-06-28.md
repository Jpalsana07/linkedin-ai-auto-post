# LinkedIn Post

Topic: LLM Cost Optimization

Date: 2026-06-28

---

One prompt cost $18.

I was building an internal support bot for a fintech team.
It answered employee questions from policy docs, and the bill jumped after launch.

The bot felt smart.
The invoices did not.

We were sending every question to GPT-4o, OpenAI’s general model, plus the full chat history on each turn.
That meant a simple “How do I reset my password?” was carrying 20 previous messages, three pasted policy pages, and a lot of wasted tokens.

Tokens are the chunks of text a model reads.
More chunks means more cost.

I tried caching answers in Redis, a fast memory store.
Helped a little.

I tried shrinking the context window, the amount of text the model sees.
Helped more.

The real problem was upstream.
We had no routing layer, the step that picks the cheapest model for the job.

Password resets did not need GPT-4o.
They needed a smaller model and a clean lookup against the policy index, the searchable doc list.

After that change, our average response dropped from $0.42 to $0.06.
Same bot.
Same users.
Different bill.

The lesson: don’t optimize prompts first.
Optimize what asks the model to think at all.

# LinkedIn Post

Topic: LLM Cost Optimization

Date: 2026-07-11

---

Cheap prompts are a false economy.

The popular advice is simple:
send less text, use a smaller model, and the bill will drop.

That helped us for a while.

Then we shipped an AI support workflow and the token bill still hit $4,800 in one month.
Most of the cost was not the prompt.
It was repeated retries, long chat histories, and bad routing to the wrong model.

We stopped obsessing over prompt length.
Instead, we used LangChain, a tool for chaining model steps, to route easy tickets to a smaller model and reserve the expensive one for messy cases.

Cost dropped 37%.
Response quality stayed flat.

The lesson: LLM cost is usually a systems problem, not a prompt problem.

The strongest counterargument is real:
sometimes prompt trimming is the fastest win, especially when you are early and the usage is tiny.

I still trim prompts when it is easy.

But if your bill keeps climbing, look at the shape of the workflow before you start shaving words.

Where have you seen the real cost hide: the prompt, or the system around it?

# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Startup Lessons

Date: 2026-07-08

---

I’m building an AI triage tool for support teams.
It helps small teams answer customer tickets faster.

This week I shipped the first inbox import, so a team can pull in 1,247 old tickets and see them in one place.
That mattered because nobody wants to test a support tool on fake data.

The good news: first response time dropped from 14 minutes to 4 minutes in our test queue.
The bad news: 19% of imported threads lost their internal notes.
That means the agent sees the customer story, but not the private context the team used to rely on.

I spent two days blaming the importer.

It turned out the real issue was simpler.
I was treating every message like the same kind of message.
A customer reply, an internal note, and a system event are not the same thing.
If you flatten them too early, you lose the thread.

So the lesson this week was blunt:
shipping fast only helps if you preserve the shape of the real thing.

What’s the hardest part of keeping real-world context intact when you build?

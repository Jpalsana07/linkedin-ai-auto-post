# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Freelancing with Numbers

Date: 2026-07-17

---

Freelancers love hiding rates.

The market says: don’t talk numbers until the client is hooked.
Keep your price flexible.
Let the call “build trust.”

I used to do that.

Then I started putting a number in the first email.
Not a full quote.
Just a range: $8k to $12k for the first project.

One client disappeared.

Three replied faster than ever.
One said, “Thank you for being direct.”
We closed in 6 days.

The contrarian take:
numbers do not scare good clients away.
They scare away the people shopping for a bargain.

The strongest counterargument is real:
if you name a price too early, you can anchor yourself too low.
I’ve done that too.

But vague pricing costs more.
It burns hours on calls, invites scope creep, and turns a simple project into a negotiation.

The lesson I keep relearning:
clarity is a filter.

What number do you think should come first in a freelance conversation?

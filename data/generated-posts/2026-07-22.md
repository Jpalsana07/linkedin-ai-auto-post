# LinkedIn Post

Topic: LLM in Production

Date: 2026-07-22

---

The bot sounded confident.

We were building an internal LLM assistant for a support team of 40 people.

It answered tickets fast, until one Monday it confidently invented a refund policy that didn’t exist.

We had wired it to a retrieval system — a search box for documents — and assumed that was enough.

It wasn’t.

The model was getting old policy docs, a stale FAQ, and one half-written note from Slack.

The real failure wasn’t the model.

It was our context.

We treated all sources like they were equally trustworthy, so the bot mixed official policy with random chatter.

We fixed it by labeling sources, adding freshness checks, and blocking Slack notes unless a human approved them.

After that, the hallucinations dropped from 17 a day to 2.

The lesson: an LLM in production is only as good as the information you let it see.

If you feed it noise, it will answer with confidence anyway.

What source would you trust first if a bot had to answer for your team?

# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Career Inflection Moments

Date: 2026-07-03

---

I almost shipped the shortcut.

We were building onboarding for a small SaaS.
Every new user had to connect Stripe, import data, and finish setup fast.

The choice was simple on paper:
build one flexible flow now,
or split it into 3 separate steps.

The flexible version looked cleaner.
One screen, fewer buttons, less code.

But the tradeoff was hidden.
If anything failed, users would lose the whole flow.
And in the first week, 31% of signups were coming from people on weak laptops and shaky Wi‑Fi.

So I chose the boring option.
Three steps.
More clicks.
Less chance of a half-finished setup getting wiped out.

That decision cost us polish.
It saved us from support tickets we would have spent months untangling.

The lesson I keep relearning:
the best architecture is often the one that survives bad internet, tired users, and rushed decisions.

That tradeoff doesn’t apply when the task is truly tiny.
If the whole job takes 30 seconds, friction matters more than failure recovery.

What tradeoff do you think founders ignore most when they’re trying to make a product feel “simple”?

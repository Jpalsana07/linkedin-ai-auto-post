# LinkedIn Post

Topic: LLM in Production

Date: 2026-06-26

---

This week, the bot got quieter.

I’m building an LLM support assistant for a SaaS team.
It answers customer questions from help docs and past tickets.

Last week, it was fast but sloppy.
This week, I shipped a stricter retrieval layer, which is the part that finds the right source text before the model answers.

That changed one number that mattered:
wrong-answer rate dropped from 18% to 6%.

I used OpenAI for generation and Postgres for stored tickets.
Postgres is our database, the place we keep records.

The win looked clean on a chart.
The messy part was obvious in Slack.

The bot now says “I don’t know” more often.
That sounds worse until you watch support agents.
They trust a cautious answer.
They do not trust a confident guess.

What’s still broken?
Latency jumped from 1.8 seconds to 3.4 seconds on long questions.
The model is waiting on a second search pass, and some replies still miss old tickets with weird wording.

So the lesson this week is simple:
in production, a slower honest answer beats a fast fake one.

What would you ship first: speed, or trust?

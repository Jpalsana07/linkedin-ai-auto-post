Topic: AI Evals

Date: 2026-06-19

---

I stopped trusting vibe checks.

We were building an AI support bot, and the real problem was simple: how do we tell if it’s actually helping people, or just sounding confident?

That meant comparing two eval tools: LangSmith, the tracing and testing tool from LangChain, and OpenAI Evals, OpenAI’s benchmark runner.

LangSmith was faster to set up.
We had traces on the same day, and could see exactly where answers drifted across 200 customer tickets.

OpenAI Evals was stricter.
It took longer to wire in, but it forced us to write clean test cases and score the model the same way every run.

After 2 weeks, the winner was clear.

LangSmith helped us debug.
OpenAI Evals helped us decide.

When we needed one source of truth for whether a change was better or worse, OpenAI Evals won.
It was less pretty, but more honest.

The lesson: use the tool that makes bad news obvious.
Pretty dashboards are nice.
Repeatable scores keep you from fooling yourself.

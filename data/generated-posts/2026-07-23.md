# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Career Inflection Moments

Date: 2026-07-23

---

My worst career move paid off.

People say the big jump is quitting fast.
Burn the bridge. Bet on yourself. Reset the clock.

I believed that too.

So I left a safe product job at 29 for a startup that looked like a rocket ship.
It had 12 people, a messy roadmap, and one very loud investor.

Three months later, the company stalled.
I was suddenly the person explaining why the “dream role” had turned into a layoff conversation.

That sting changed how I think about career inflection moments.

The best moves are not always the boldest ones.
Sometimes they are the ones that keep optionality alive while everyone else is chasing drama.

I took a smaller title after that.
Less shiny.
More boring.
But it gave me 18 months to learn sales, sit in customer calls, and build a network that later helped me land my next founder role.

The strongest counterargument is real:
some jobs are toxic, and waiting too long can cost you years.

I still think the lesson holds.

Don’t optimize for the story you can tell on LinkedIn.
Optimize for the set of doors your next move keeps open.

What career move looked small at the time but changed everything later?

One cache saved us.

We were shipping an LLM feature that answered customer questions inside our app.

The scary part wasn’t the model.
It was the bill and the lag.

Every reply had to feel fast, but every prompt to GPT-4o Mini meant another paid API call.
At 8,000 requests a day, small delays turned into angry users and a very real monthly tab.

The decision was simple to describe:
do we cache full answers, or only cache the “retrieval” step — the part that fetches the source facts before the model writes a reply?

Full-answer caching is cheaper.
It also goes stale fast when product docs change.
Retrieval caching is less dramatic, but it keeps the facts fresh and still cuts most of the work.

We chose retrieval caching.

Why?
Because our support docs changed 3–5 times a week.
A stale answer with a fast response is still a bad answer.

So we cached the facts, not the finished sentence.

The lesson: when you can’t afford to be wrong, cache the part that changes least.

This tradeoff breaks down if your content barely changes, or if you need exact repeatability for compliance.

What would you cache first in an LLM app: the answer, or the facts?

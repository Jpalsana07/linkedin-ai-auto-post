# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Node.js at Scale

Date: 2026-07-15

---

We stopped sharing one Node process.

That sounded boring.
It saved our API.

At 40k requests a minute, one slow image resize could freeze the whole app.
A customer would click “checkout,” and their page would sit behind someone else’s export job.

The decision was simple: keep web traffic and background jobs separate.

The tradeoff was real.
One codebase is easier to ship.
Two processes mean more moving parts, more deploys, and a little more ops work.

We chose separation because our worst bottleneck was not code size.
It was queue starvation — when background work eats the workers your users need right now.

So we ran the API in Node.js and pushed heavy jobs into BullMQ, the Redis-based job queue.
That let us keep checkout fast even when a batch import ran for 12 minutes.

The lesson: when users are waiting, protect the path they touch first.
Everything else can be slower.

If you were splitting one Node app today, what would you isolate first?

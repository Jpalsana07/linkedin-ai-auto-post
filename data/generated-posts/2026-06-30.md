Topic: AI Agents

Date: 2026-06-30

---

I trusted the wrong memory.

We were building an AI agent to help our support team answer refund requests.
If it worked, it would save hours a day and keep angry customers from waiting.

The agent kept sounding confident.
It even had access to the right policy docs.

But it still made up details.

The mistake was simple:
I treated the model like a person who could remember the rules.
It isn’t.

An agent is only as good as the instructions, tools, and checks around it.
Without a hard source of truth, it will fill gaps with something that sounds right.

We fixed it by forcing every refund answer through one live policy lookup before the reply.
After that change, the bad answers dropped to almost zero.

The lesson I keep relearning:
don’t ask an agent to remember what it can read.

# LinkedIn Post

Topic: Agent Reliability

Date: 2026-07-20

---

I’m building an AI support agent.

It’s for a small SaaS team that answers customer tickets.

This week I shipped retries, tool timeouts, and a hard stop after 3 failed actions.

Before that, the agent would wander.

It could draft a good reply, then try to check billing, then get stuck waiting on a slow API rate limit — the max requests a service allows — and burn the whole run.

After the update, the success rate on real tickets went from 61% to 84%.

Average response time dropped from 18 seconds to 9.

That sounds great until you hit the broken part.

The agent is still brittle on messy tickets with two asks in one message.

If a customer says “refund me and reset my password,” it often solves one and drops the other.

The lesson is simple: reliability is not making the agent smarter.

It’s making failure smaller, faster, and easier to notice.

That’s the part I keep shipping now.

What would you fix first: the agent’s memory, its tool calls, or its fallback behavior?

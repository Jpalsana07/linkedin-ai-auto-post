Topic: AI Evals

Date: 2026-07-02

---

The evals finally caught it.

I’m building an AI support copilot for a small SaaS team.

The goal is simple: help agents answer faster without making up facts.

This week I shipped an eval set of 120 real customer questions, plus a grading script in Python that scores each reply for correctness, tone, and hallucinations.

Before that, we were guessing.

A change looked good in a demo, then failed on the weird edge cases customers actually ask.

After the evals, one model jumped from 68% to 84% on answer quality.

That number sounds good until you see the gap:
it still missed refund policy questions and kept giving confident but wrong account steps.

The broken part is not the model alone.

It’s our eval coverage.

We had plenty of “happy path” questions and almost no angry, messy, half-written tickets from real users.

So the lesson this week was clear:
if you only test the easy cases, your AI will look smart right up until it meets a customer.

What do you think is harder to catch early: wrong answers, or confident wrong answers?
